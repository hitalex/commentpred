---3------------------------------------------------The Evolution of Freedom of Information 信息自由演进历程 1776 Sweden is believed to be the first country to adopt freedom of information legislation with its Freedom of Information Act of 1776. 1776年，瑞典率先通过《信息自由法案》，成为第一个为信息自由立法的国家。 Today, some 80 countries have implemented some sort of freedom of information legislation. 今天，全球已有八十多个国家制定了类似的信息自由法案。 1822 James Madison, the fourth U.S. president and author of the Bill of Rights, cited the importance of freedom of information for the preservation of democracy. In an 1822 letter, he wrote: “A popular government without popular information, or the means of acquiring it, is but a prologue to a farce or a tragedy; or perhaps both. Knowledge will forever govern ignorance; and the people who mean to be their own governors must arm themselves with the power which knowledge gives.” 作为《人权法案》的撰写者之一，第四届美国总统詹姆斯•麦迪逊曾经这样肯定信息自由对于维护民主的重要性：在一封成于1822年的信中他这样写到，“一个当时的政府，如果没有当时的信息，或者没有获取当时信息的方法，它只能是一幕滑稽剧或一场悲剧的开场白，或许两样都是。知识总比无知高出一头，人民要做自己的主人，必须先用知识赋予的能力来武装自己。” 1955 The first U.S. congressional hearings on availability of information from federal agencies began on November 7, 1955, before the House Special Subcommittee on Government Information. At first there was much skepticism about the legislation. Many officials felt the transparency called for by the freedom of information concept would constrain them from communicating sensitive information, thereby hampering government functions. 1955年11月7日，美国议会赶在众议院政府信息特别委员会前面，第一次就从联邦机构获取信息可行性的议题进行听证。最初人们关于该立法案项疑虑颇多，很多官员担心，信息自由概念所对应的信息透明度将约束官员们就敏感信息进行交流的活动，因而有碍政府部门运作。 -------------------------------------- 我被卡在了1966年。等全篇译完再比照别人的版本。真是羞于贴出来见人呢~ --------------------------- 魔鬼小语：艺术成于苦痛。 Spirit note: Art explains the anguish.
